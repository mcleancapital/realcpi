The DXY (U.S. Dollar Index) is a measure of the value of the U.S. dollar relative to a basket of foreign currencies. It provides a broad indication of the dollar's strength in global markets.

The DXY is a trade-weighted index based on the exchange rates of six major currencies:
  • Currency Weight (%)
  • Euro (EUR) 57.6%
  • Japanese Yen (JPY) 13.6%
  • British Pound (GBP) 11.9%
  • Canadian Dollar (CAD) 9.1%
  • Swedish Krona (SEK) 4.2%
  • Swiss Franc (CHF) 3.6%
